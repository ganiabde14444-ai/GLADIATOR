Program Intent

Forging the shield

The Iron Phalanx is not a standard gym routine. It is a disciplined progression model focused on force production, bone density, trunk stability, and movement under load. Olympic-lift variations, heavy compounds, and carry work are used to create a body that can express power and survive pressure.

Weekly Regiment

Four sessions with four clear jobs

Monday Heavy lower body Squat emphasis, posterior-chain volume, and bracing work.
Wednesday Heavy upper body Press focus with aggressive upper-back and grip support.
Friday Full body hypertrophy Density circuits and loaded carries to build armor.
Saturday Max effort / test exposure Top-set execution, PR attempts, or quality benchmark work.
Pillar 01

Power

Low-rep, high-output work raises your ceiling on the big lifts and improves bar speed under pressure.

Pillar 02

Armor

Grip, trunk, and upper-back volume make heavy loading more stable and repeatable across blocks.

Pillar 03

Capacity

Conditioning is programmed to support recovery and work tolerance without flattening strength gains.
